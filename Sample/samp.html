            The sons of James Cook descend, flies
            in double-breasted suits, crossover ties.
            
            They come forth with cars, sallow tourists,
            hand saws eager to gather in the harvest
            
            of statues, ripped like Acacia fingernails
            from Rongo’s bountiful hands. Corpses are piled
            
            and baptized in the tears of Lono.
            Moro, Tarocco, Sanguinello—
            
            say the names. The nectar is sweet,
            titian, but bitter. Your tongue will taste the teeth
            
            that tore the scalps like a crocodile
            discovering a swimmer.
            
                                                           The wahine smiles
